About Me

I’m a student, early-career product manager, and possibly one of the biggest extroverts you'll meet.

Currently studying Systems Design Engineering @ UWaterloo. When I’m not knee deep in non-fiction, catch me going on long walks or playing (a vast assortment of) word games! I can also be found feverishly analyzing basketball plays, learning a new viola concerto, or diving into media about good urban design.

I always feel at home meeting people who are curious, infectiously driven, and lightly encased by wit. If any of these tickle your fancy, reach out and let’s chat!

Some of my favourite reads are...

When Breath Becomes Air by Paul Kalanithi

  • Memoir

One of the first memoirs I've read. Paul brings in eye-opening perspectives as physician-patient. I actually found the afterword, written by his wife, to be most touching. Would recommend to anyone.

Inspired by Marty Cagan

  • Product Management

The book that grew my love for product management. I also recommend reading Marty's other book, Empowered.

They Said This Would Be Fun by Eternity Martis

  • Memoir

Wrote a (quite long) essay spotlighting social justice issues at Canadian institutions, which you can find here.

Educated by Tara Westover

  • Memoir

A fascinating memoir. Did some research and found out that Tara's mother is trying to fund her own book to give her own perspective - would be an intriguing read for sure.

The Design of Everyday Things by Don Norman

  • Design
  • Psychology

Ties in pretty nicely with my program and would be a great read in our human-computer interaction course.
